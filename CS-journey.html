I started this all because of a simple CAPTCHA. To address this more accurately, my interest in embarking on a journey in Computer Science stems from the early years of my career as a QA engineer. While writing a routine automation test for a login page on a website, I noticed that the test run could not proceed any further, simply because of a CAPTCHA which was at the bottom of the page. This made me curious to research about CAPTCHA and its underlying technology. This exploration led me to understand the principles of image processing, pattern recognition, and optical character recognition (OCR) that underpin CAPTCHA systems. Moving on, I thought to myself if a robot/machine could learn the details of a picture and make a decision based on that, then what are the other applications of this magnificent technology and its applications. Fascinated by this technology, I delved into the realm of Augmented Reality (AR) and Virtual Reality (VR) and continued the research. Regarding that I hold 2 years of experience in the game industry as a Game designer and a QA Engineer, I knew for a fact that if I could dive deeper into Computer Vision as an academic study, I could understand and utilize this technology in a more advanced sense. Ultimately, I would be able to apply what I have gained, into the development of more immersive and realistic games utilizing computer vision, specifically AR and VR.
